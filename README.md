# artinscience · cathedrals

**Anatomy of a Cathedral** — the story of a single great building (Gothic
cathedral or castle), its **creation and/or destruction**, grounded in
historical evidence. Architectural drawing (plans, elevations, sections,
exploded cutaways) is among the most plotter-native subjects there is.

**The drawing as the building's own method:** medieval masons designed by
compass-and-straightedge (*ad quadratum* / *ad triangulum*), rose-window
tracery, vault ribs — reproduced as generative geometry, the art *is* the
historical construction method.

**Building candidates:** Notre-Dame de Paris (creation → 2019 fire → rebuild),
Beauvais (overreach & collapse), Cologne (632-year build), Château Gaillard.

**Chapter spine:** construction phases (foundation → nave → buttresses → vaults →
spire) **or** a timeline of rise & ruin. Anchored in fabric account rolls, HABS /
Viollet-le-Duc surveys, dendrochronology, photogrammetry.
